Hub airports are useful places to study delay propagation because they concentrate aircraft, crews, passengers, and connecting itineraries. A disruption at one large hub can quickly become a network-level issue, especially when the same carrier uses that airport as a scheduling anchor.

Regional airports face a different reliability problem. They may not generate the original disruption, but they often have fewer alternate flights, smaller buffers, and less schedule redundancy. A missed connection or late inbound aircraft can create a delay pattern that is small in volume but large in passenger impact.

For analysis, this means delay counts alone are not enough. I want to compare delay frequency, route dependency, recovery time, and airport role within the broader network.
