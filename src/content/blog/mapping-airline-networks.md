---
title: Mapping Airline Networks as Geographic Systems
description: Airline route maps become more useful when they show geography, hierarchy, hub structure, and regional access rather than only origin-destination lines.
pubDate: 2026-01-04
tags: ["GIS", "networks", "visualization"]
---

Airline networks are geographic systems as much as graph systems. A route is an edge, but it is also a connection across terrain, population centers, airport catchment areas, and regional economies.

A useful map should make hierarchy visible. Hub airports, focus cities, thin regional routes, and dense corridors all behave differently. If every route line receives the same visual weight, the map can become impressive but difficult to interpret.

My preferred approach is to combine network metrics with geographic context: route frequency, airport role, distance, carrier concentration, and regional access. This makes the map a tool for asking better questions rather than just a picture of many lines.
