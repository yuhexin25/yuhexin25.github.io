## Summary

Cirium's 2025 Flight Emissions Review compares airlines by emissions intensity, measured in grams of CO2 per available seat kilometer. The review is useful because it treats sustainability as an operational measurement problem, not just a general environmental claim.

## Key Findings

- Scoot ranked first at 51.0 grams of CO2 per available seat kilometer.
- Wizz Air ranked second at 52.9g, followed by TUI Airways at 53.6g, Air Europa at 53.9g, and Frontier Airlines at 54.1g.
- Fleet age and seat density are major drivers of emissions intensity.
- Route length, aircraft age, aircraft configuration, engine variant, airspace availability, and routing efficiency can also affect emissions.

## Supporting Evidence

Cirium states that "fleet age and seat density remain the two factors that matter most," but the review also notes that "airspace availability, routing efficiency and air traffic management all contribute." This is important because it links airline-level choices, such as fleet renewal and cabin configuration, with route-level and system-level constraints.

## My Reflection

This report connects sustainability to network thinking. An aircraft's emissions performance depends on the aircraft itself, but it also depends on where and how it flies. For future aviation dashboards, emissions should be mapped alongside route distance, aircraft type, airport pairs, airspace constraints, and operational efficiency.
